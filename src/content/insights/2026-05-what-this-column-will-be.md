Most consultancies treat their blog as a content marketing channel:
SEO bait, predictions, top-ten lists, generic "how AI is transforming
X" essays. We are not going to do that here.

Working in Public is the editorial program at Kitty Kat Technologies.
Two named authors. One post a month each. The cadence is the contract
— if a post is not ready, it is not shipped, but the next month is.

## What we will publish

Notes from inside engagements: what we shipped, what we did not, what
we changed our minds about. Sharp observations from real production
work — at Alfa Oil, in retail engagements, around Optimus, on the
delivery model. Pieces where the author is identifiable and the claim
is specific.

When something we tried did not work, we will say so. When a number
moved, we will name the range and the conditions. When we revised a
prior position, the post will say *we used to think X — here is why
that was wrong*.

## What we will not publish

No "5 ways AI will transform retail" listicles. No predictions about
where AI is going. No vendor takes on tools we have not shipped with.
No anonymous case stories. No content written to rank for keywords.

## Cadence

The column opens with this post. The first substantive piece lands as
soon as it is ready — likely in the next four to six weeks, drawn
from a recent retail engagement. Subscribe via RSS at
`https://kittykat.tech/rss.xml` if that is your habit. Otherwise the
new posts surface on `/insights` and from the `Insights` link in the
footer.

If the cadence slips for two consecutive months, we will say so on
this page and adjust the contract. No silent decay.
