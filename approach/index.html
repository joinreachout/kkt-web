Approach

A delivery model, not a deck.

We work in three stages. Most clients enter at stage one or two — they think they need stage three. The diagnostic tells us which one is real.

14
days, fixed-shape diagnostic
3
stages, optional past stage 2
1
senior internal counterpart, always
01

Strengthen the core

Data, governance, decision-grade visibility. The unsexy work that makes everything after it possible.

02

Optimize and grow

AI and PMO discipline applied where they earn their place — margin, cash, availability, customer value, productivity.

03

Expand and monetize

New revenue, supplier monetization, customer ownership, strategic expansion. The stage where the system runs without us.

PMO is the rigor.

We are not an AI vendor. We are a delivery firm with deep AI capability. PMO discipline — scope, sequencing, ownership, shipping under constraint — is what separates a slide-led project from one that runs in production.

If the case for an engagement is not there, we say so at the readout. Honest answers beat a sold project.

Inside a diagnostic

Two weeks. One sharp picture.

Every engagement we accept starts with a diagnostic. Two weeks, fixed-shape, focused on a single output: a sharp picture of what to ship, what to leave alone, and what proof you need before committing to the next stage.

14
days, fixed shape
  1. Days 1–3

    Kick-off and access.

    Stakeholder map, data access, scope freeze. We name the business question we will answer in two weeks, and the three or four data pulls we need to answer it.

  2. Days 4–9

    Investigation.

    Interviews with the people closest to the operating reality. Hands-on data review. Surfacing the patterns that show up across both. We tell you what we are seeing as we see it — no surprise reveal at the end.

  3. Days 10–12

    Synthesis.

    What to ship, what to leave alone, what proof to commit. Sequenced backlog with effort and impact ranges. The exit criteria for whichever stage you actually need before you reach for stage three.

  4. Day 14

    Readout.

    Concrete recommendation, presented to the senior team. Decision-ready: enough detail to commit, scoped to avoid over-promising. If the case for an engagement is not there, we say so.

After the diagnostic.

If the case is there, we agree on a sequenced engagement: a set of operating outcomes, the milestones that prove them, and the senior counterpart inside your organisation who inherits the system as we ship it. Engagement length is shaped by what is real to ship, not by a default contract length.

The exit criterion is built in from week one. When the system is running and the counterpart is operating it, we leave.
