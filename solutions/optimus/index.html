Solutions — Optimus

Optimus.

Daily operating intelligence for fuel networks.

Optimus is an operating-intelligence system for fuel-distribution networks. Each morning it pulls live data from ERP — current stocks, in-transit orders, station-level sales, supplier offers — forecasts when each station × fuel-type position will hit critical level, detects inbound delivery conflicts where arriving volume exceeds tank capacity, and produces concrete procurement recommendations: which supplier, how many tons, by what date, at what price.

A morning at the operator's desk

Ten to fifteen minutes, then the day starts.

  1. 1
    8:00 AM — Open the briefing.

    The head of supply opens Optimus and sees yesterday's pipeline freshness in one glance, not 40 minutes of reconciliation. Action Required. Good News. Savings Tip. What changed since yesterday.

  2. 2
    Close critical positions.

    Procurement Advisor surfaces every station × fuel that will hit critical level inside the planning window. Each row already has a recommended supplier, tonnage, and deadline. Confirm or override.

  3. 3
    Resolve delivery conflicts.

    When inbound volume exceeds available tank capacity, Auto-Distribute reroutes overflow to the nearest stations with free capacity. The operator sees the proposed routing, not just an alert.

  4. 4
    Issue the POs.

    One-click PO creation on confirmed recommendations. The Reserves Panel covers any fuel already held at supplier without destination — assign it to a station with capacity check.

  5. 5
    Walk away.

    Critical alerts mirror to a Telegram group. The operator does not depend on the web interface for the rest of the day.

What it actually changes

Outcomes, not features.

Stockouts

Fewer surprise critical positions. The forecast surfaces them days before they happen, when there is still time to act.

Working capital

$ frozen in inventory becomes visible per fuel and per region — and so does where to release it. Inventory turnover stops being a quarterly review and becomes a daily decision.

Procurement speed

Decisions that took half a day of reconciliation move to minutes. The operator sees the recommendation and the data behind it on the same screen.

Supplier consolidation

Recommendation history makes supplier-by-supplier savings visible over time. The Procurement Advisor's defaults shift with what the data shows.

How it works

The full functional surface, in four clusters.

Fourteen capabilities, organised by how the operator actually meets them — what you see, what you act on, what you track over time, and what runs without you.

Visibility

What you see on day one.

  • AI Briefing

    Plain-language daily summary — Action Required, Good News, Savings Tip. Free-form chat answers questions from live data.

  • Stockout Forecast

    Days remaining until critical level per station × fuel. Includes in-transit and split-wagon transfers.

  • Fuel Level Forecast

    Per-station stock projection charts with region and fuel filters.

  • Station Fill Levels

    Per-station, per-fuel % capacity and tonnage, sorted by urgency.

Decision surface

What you act on.

  • Procurement Advisor

    Concrete order recommendations with supplier, tonnage, deadline, price. Statuses CRITICAL / ORDER / OK. One-click PO creation.

  • Reserves Panel

    Fuel held at supplier without destination. Release reserve assigns to a specific station with capacity check.

  • Delivery conflict resolver

    Auto-Distribute reroutes overflow wagons to nearest stations with free capacity. Audit trail.

  • Forced Order flow

    Senior management override path with audit trail and ship-history fallback.

History & money

What you track over time.

  • Working Capital

    $ frozen in inventory and where to release.

  • Inventory Turnover

    Per-fuel turnover rate, days on hand, value at stake.

  • Recommendation History

    Daily snapshots — rewind to any past day to see what was advised and what happened.

  • Shipments Timeline

    14 / 30 / 60-day forward view per station. Color-coded OK / Tight / Conflict.

Autonomous

What runs without you.

  • Auto-import (cron, ~03:30 local)

    Pulls fresh stocks and orders from ERP. No manual upload.

  • Telegram alert mirror

    Critical events pushed to operator group regardless of web availability.

Strategy & Scenarios

Five operating modes, one engine.

Optimus runs the same forecast engine in five postures — pick the one that matches the moment. The procurement recommendations, working-capital pressure, and inventory posture re-balance accordingly. CFO-grade comparison built into the operator surface.

Standard Safety

Default. Conservative buffers, 45-day horizon, low surprise risk.

Cost Optimization

Push for cheapest landed price across the network. Tighter buffers, more sensitivity to supplier price shifts.

Cash Conservation

Minimise capital lock. Lower inventory days, smaller PO sizes, accept slightly tighter operating margin.

Balanced

Median posture between cost, cash, and risk. Useful default when conditions are stable.

Crisis

Hold-the-line mode. Survive the immediate window, defer everything that can be deferred.

Each mode produces a different recommendation set against the same operational reality — visible side-by-side. Mode comparison view in the screenshot above.
